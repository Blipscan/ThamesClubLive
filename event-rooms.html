Private dining, corporate gatherings, weddings, receptions, and occasions that deserve a room with a hundred and fifty years of good conversation in the walls.

The Rooms

Spaces with
real character.

The Thames Club is not a hotel ballroom. It is a house — with a library, a formal dining room, a hunt room with fireplaces, and a distinctive basement pub with two duckpin lanes. Each space brings something a generic venue cannot.

Main Dining Room

The Dining Room

The Club's primary gathering space. Formal meals, receptions, and private dinners for up to 60 guests. Fireplace, full dining service, and a room that has hosted New London's civic and social life for over a century.

The Library

Floor-to-ceiling shelves, a fireplace, and the kind of quiet that makes conversations worth having. Ideal for smaller gatherings — board dinners, cocktail receptions, literary events, and intimate occasions.

The Hunt Room
