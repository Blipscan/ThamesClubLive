The Thames Club · New London

Dining at
the Club

The table has always been at the center of this house. Member dining, private events, seasonal menus — served in rooms that have held good meals since 1905.

The Dining Room

A room built
for the table.

The Thames Club dining room is the social center of the house. It has held formal dinners, working lunches, celebratory banquets, and the quiet Tuesday night meals that are the real fabric of club life.

Dining is a member privilege. Guests are welcome at member tables. The kitchen serves lunch and dinner on regular days; the full dining program is available on request for private events.

The dining room is not a restaurant. It is a room that knows its members.

Private Dining

Your occasion,
our house.

The Thames Club is available for private dining events — from small dinners to full room buyouts. The event planning team manages the details. Members and non-members may reserve the Club for private occasions.

Small Dinner

Private table for 4–12. Full menu service, wine selection by arrangement. The most intimate way to use the Club.

From $45 per person + service

Private Room

Seated dinner for up to 40 in the private dining room. Custom menu available. Ideal for corporate dinners, milestone occasions, and family gatherings.

From $65 per person + service

Full Club Buyout

Exclusive use of the entire Club — dining room, bar, social rooms. Reception and seated dinner service for up to 120 guests.

By quotation

Bar & Cellar

A proper drink
before dinner.

The Club bar is a member amenity — cocktails, a curated wine list, and a selection of spirits appropriate to a house this old. Beer on draft. Bottles by the glass or by the bottle. Bar service begins at four o'clock on regular days.

The wine program emphasizes American producers, with particular attention to coastal appellations and New England growers. The list changes with the menu.

Wine by the glass begins at nine dollars. Bottles from forty. A selection of cellar-aged bottles is available by request.

Sunday Brunch

The slow
Sunday meal.

Sunday brunch is served on selected Sundays through the season — late morning through early afternoon. The menu is relaxed: eggs in several forms, smoked fish, pastry from a local baker, and a Bloody Mary made with the attention it deserves.

Dates are announced in the monthly newsletter and on the events calendar. Members may bring guests. Reservations required.

Hours & Reservations

When and
how to dine.

Dining is open to members and accompanied guests. Reservations are encouraged for dinner; walk-ins are welcome at lunch when the dining room is not reserved for a private event. The kitchen closes at nine-thirty.

For private dining and event inquiries, contact the Club directly. The Event Planning team works with members and outside clients on custom menus, staffing, and arrangements.
